Important Grammar Tips

Can you conjugate this verb?

Give it a go. Take a look at the endings for the so called "regular verbs" of the German language:


Present Tense of "machen":
ich ...-e - ich mache
du ...st - du machst
er, sie, es ...-t - er, sie, es macht
wir ...-en - wir machen
ihr ...-t - ihr macht
sie, Sie ...-en - sie/Sie machen


Now that you know the endings of these regular verbs, try to do as many conjugations as you can, in order to get a good grasp on the principles.

Look at the conjugation of the verb haben on the right side. Let's try to build the German present perfect by using the conjugated version of haben, which here is a helping verb and adding the past participle of "machen" which is "gemacht". Like this: ich habe ... gemacht

The verbs haben & sein

Now let us have a look at the Present Tense and the Simple Past (Präteritum) forms of the most important verbs in the German language. Note that these two verbs are also "helping verbs". This means that upon building another tense like Present Perfect these verbs are required. Haben is used for static actions while sein is used for dynamic actions.


Present Tense of "haben":
ich habe = I have
du hast
er, sie, es hat
wir haben = We have
ihr habt
sie, Sie haben


Simple Past Tense of "haben":
ich hatte = I had
du hattest
er, sie, es hatte
wir hatten = We had
ihr hattet
sie, Sie hatten


Present Tense of "sein":
ich bin = I am
du bist
er, sie, es ist
wir sind = We are
ihr seid
sie, Sie sind


Simple Past Tense of "sein":
ich war = I was
du warst
er, sie, es war
wir waren = We were
ihr wart
sie, Sie waren
